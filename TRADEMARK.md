## 3. Permitted Uses Without Written Permission

Subject to the style rules in Section 7, You may use Woodfine Marks
without separate written permission for:

  (a) Nominative fair use — accurately referring to Woodfine's
      products or services by their mark (e.g., "works with PointSav,"
      "built on PointSav," "compatible with PointSav").

  (b) Attribution required by a Woodfine license — where a license
      issued by Woodfine (PointSav-ARR, PointSav Commercial, or any
      open-source license Woodfine applies to material it publishes)
      requires attribution, the minimum use of the mark necessary for
      that attribution is permitted.

  (c) Truthful factual reference — journalism, academic writing,
      commentary, criticism, review, and parody may refer to Woodfine
      Marks accurately without permission.

  (d) Identifying a fork or derivative — contributors to repositories
      Woodfine has licensed under AGPLv3, FSL-1.1, or Apache-2.0 may
      identify their work as "a fork of," "derived from," or "based
      on" a Woodfine product, provided they do not imply that
      Woodfine endorses or supports the fork.

## 7. Style and Usage Rules

When using any Woodfine Mark under Section 3 or under a separate
written permission:

  (a) The first use of a mark in a document or web page should
      display the mark with a ™ symbol (e.g., "PointSav™").
      Subsequent references in the same document or page may omit
      the symbol.

  (b) Use the mark as an adjective modifying a generic noun (e.g.,
      "the PointSav platform," "Woodfine offerings"). Do not use the
      mark as a verb ("PointSav'd") or pluralize it ("PointSavs").

  (c) Do not incorporate any Woodfine Mark into Your own trademark,
      trade name, product name, or logo.

  (d) Do not display any Woodfine Mark more prominently than Your
      own marks or primary content.

  (e) Include the attribution "PointSav is a trademark of Woodfine
      Capital Projects Inc." (or the equivalent for other marks)
      where reasonable, particularly in marketing contexts covered
      by Section 4.

misuse does not waive its right to enforce against other misuse.
